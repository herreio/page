Curriculum vitae
(Apr 23, 2019)

After graduating from a BA in German Literature (major) and Philosophy (minor) at the Humboldt University of Berlin with a thesis »On the Use of Photography in Walter Benjamin’s Thinking and Writing«, I am now studying Digital Humanities at the University of Leipzig.

Besides, I am assisting the library of the Max Planck Institute for Social Anthropology in Halle (Saale) by managing their publication data. Find my recently released items here.

In the past years, I have worked for the German Opera Berlin (voluntary cultural year), the chair of Prof. Dr. Andreas Polaschegg at the Humboldt University (student assistant), the Mosse-Lectures (student assistant) and the Henschel Verlag (internship).

During my humanities studies, I have written assignments and papers about T. Hobbes, Spinoza, J. G. Herder, F. Schiller, G. W. F. Hegel, W. James, F. de Saussure, L. Wittgenstein, W. Benjamin, N. Goodman and A. Kluge, while covering subjects such as aesthetics, cinematography, digital media, epistemology, literary criticism, media theory, metaphysics, painting, philosophy of history, philosophy of language, philosophy of religion, photography, political philosophy, psychology, sculpture and semiotics.

During my digital humanities studies, I have written code in C, Java, Lisp, Prolog, Python and R. Also I have worked on a project which made use of the Facebook Graph API to analyze the social network of German political parties and news outlets, as well as on a project which made use of the Telegram API to crawl news feeds from media outlets and build a corpus. Furthermore, I have annotated textual data with POS and NER tags by using machine learning techniques, experimented with modeling topics of (short) texts using the Latent Dirichlet Allocation and last but not least gained experience in working with the Canonical Text Service.

Right now, I am preparing my BSc thesis which will deal with the provision, management and analysis of publication data.
